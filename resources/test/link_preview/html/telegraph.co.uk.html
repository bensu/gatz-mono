Gaza faces a perpetual war if Israel and Hamas do not sign a hostage deal to end the conflict, the US warned last night.

Antony Blinken, the US Secretary of State, said Washington estimated that Hamas had recruited almost as many new members as it had lost during the war against Israel so far.

Each time Israel completes its military operations and pulls back Hamas, militants regroup and re-emerge because theres nothing else to fill the void.

Indeed, we assess that Hamas has recruited almost as many new militants as it has lost, Mr Blinken said in a speech at the Atlantic Council on Tuesday.

That is a recipe for an enduring insurgency and perpetual war, he added.

It came as he unveiled his plan for what happens to Gaza after the war ends, including foreign militaries being deployed to keep the peace before a new Palestinian administration is formed and trained.

Earlier on Tuesday, sources said that Hamas had agreed to the deal already as a final round of talks took place in Qatar.

Drafts of the deal show three phases where hostages will be released in batches and exchanged for Hamas prisoners until the conditions are met for a full withdrawal of Israeli forces from the Strip.

A delegation from the Palestinian terror group Islamic Jihad was due to arrive in the Qatari capital on Tuesday evening. The group is thought to have captured and held a number of hostages separately from Hamas.

The deal is likely to go to a vote at the Israeli security cabinet, which is reportedly broadly in favour.

Officials have been scrambling to secure a deal before Donald Trump takes over the White House on Monday. Mr Blinken said the Biden administration would hand over its plan for running Gaza.

He said the plan would ensure Hamas would not be able to play a role in the Strips future, although it was unclear what would happen to the organisation.

An interim government will be established made up of representatives from Gaza and the Palestinian Authority, which will oversee the civil affairs such as the economy, education and health.

The international system will provide support to the interim government financially and technically while a UN official will be appointed to oversee reconstruction of Gaza.

According to Mr Blinken, an interim security mission, composed of military personnel from nations allied with the US and carefully screened Palestinian personnel, would be established. This is likely to be made up of forces from Arab countries close to the US.

The mission would have the mandate of ensuring the safe delivery of humanitarian aid, maintaining border security, and preventing smuggling.

Eventually, a reformed PA would take over control of Gaza, with the US providing training and equipment to a security force under the PAs control.

The post-war framework would ultimately be enshrined in a UN Security Council resolution, Mr Blinken added.

Israelis must abandon the myth that they can carry out de facto annexation without cost and consequence to Israels democracy, to its standing and to its security, Mr Blinken said.

The plan will, however, likely be met with great scepticism in Israel as Mr Netanyahu has repeatedly refused to allow the PA to govern Gaza after the war.

The PA pays murderers and educates their children to hate Israel and, to my sorrow, to murder Jews, and ultimately for the disappearance of the State of Israel, Mr Netanyahu said at a press conference two months after Oct 7, vowing not to let this defective thing rule Gaza.

Final details of the deal were still being negotiated on Tuesday night, with Hamas claiming Israel had not provided detailed enough maps of its withdrawal from the Strip.

Mr Trump said late on Monday night that the handshakes had already been done and theyre getting it finished and maybe by the end of the week.

He has previously said there will be all hell to pay if a deal isnt signed. He sent his new Middle East envoy, the businessman Steven Witkoff, to Qatar to pressure both sides ahead of his inauguration.
